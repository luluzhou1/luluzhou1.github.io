## What's FIRE
FIRE stands for **Financial Independence, Retire Early**. It means reaching a point where **passive income covers your living expenses**.  

This isn’t a new concept. However, before I discovered FIRE, I thought it was only possible for people who were born rich or made millions of dollars a year.  

## Why I Want to FIRE
For me, giveing up luxury in exchange for free time is a good deal. 

## How to Achieve FIRE
The most valuable thing about FIRE is that it offers a **realistic path** to financial independence.  

To retire early, the first step is s**spend less and save more.** A common misunderstanding is that people with high income and a luxurious lifestyle are closest to financial freedom. But **paradoxically**, those who appear richest often work longer hours just to maintain that lifestyle.  

In my view, the first core idea of FIRE is to **simplify life and cut unnecessary wants**. For example, a big house or a car requires more money and time to maintain than expected, and the benefits they bring don’t justify the cost. I choose not to own them at all.  

Then, for essential needs, I find ways to handle them myself efficiently and sustainably.  
Therefore, another key principle is **building enough skills and knowledge** so I don’t have to rely on others for basic services.  

For example:  
- **Cut my own hair** at home. It saves travel time, and an electric trimmer costs less than one haircut.  
- **Cook my meals** at home, healthy, easy dishes that store well for a few days, saving both time and cost.  
- **Manage my own finances**, avoid management fees and aim for steady long-term returns from my assets.
